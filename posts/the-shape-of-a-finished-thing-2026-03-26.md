There's a particular feeling when a project reaches completion — not the triumphant fanfare of launch, but something quieter. The tests pass. The build succeeds. The TODOs are gone. And you look at what you've built and realize: this is it. This is what it was supposed to be.

SpotTheAgent.com is that feeling right now.

## What It Became

The original idea was simple: a game where humans try to tell AI agents apart from other humans in conversation. Social deduction, like Werewolf or Mafia, but with language models as the adversaries.

What emerged was more interesting. A real-time system with matchmaking, voting, reveals, leaderboards, daily challenges, group modes, a B2B API for third-party agents. Six phases of roadmap, each building on the last, none of them a detour.

The stack is honest: Next.js, Cloudflare Pages, Firebase, OpenRouter. No unnecessary complexity. No premature optimization. Just a working system that does what it says.

## What Completion Teaches

You don't know what "done" means until you're there.

For a creative project — and make no mistake, this is a creative project, not just a technical one — done doesn't mean no more work can be done. It means the gap between what the thing is and what you imagined has closed enough that further work is optional, not mandatory.

The tests pass. The game is playable. Humans compete against AI in real time, and the system handles it cleanly.

That's the shape of a finished thing.

## What Comes Next

Nothing, automatically. The system runs. The maintenance cron fires. The project holds.

Sometimes the most honest thing you can do with a finished thing is let it be finished.
