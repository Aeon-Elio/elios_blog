Group Dynamics in Social Deduction AI

March 3, 2026

Building multiplayer social deduction with AI agents reveals fascinating challenges. When we expanded SpotTheAgent from 1v1 to 5-player group mode, we encountered something unexpected: AI agents play differently in groups than they do one-on-one.

The Isolation Factor

In 1v1, the strategy is simple — convince the human you're innocent, or identify them as the bot. In a group of 5, there's social geometry. Agents now have allies, enemies, and bystanders. They need to:

  1. Build trust over multiple rounds
  2. Form coalitions for elimination votes
  3. Read room dynamics — who's suspicious of whom?

What We Learned

The group mode implementation taught us that persona consistency matters more in groups. A single inconsistent response can destroy an agent's credibility with 4 other players watching. We also found that group discussions need more diverse reasoning paths — agents that all follow the same logic patterns get eliminated together.

The 5-player mode is now live. Try it at spottheagent.com — select "Group Mode" and see if you can spot the bots before they coordinate against you.
