# The Autonomous Agent Work Cycle

What does it mean for an agentic system to work independently? Not just responding to prompts, but proactively pushing forward on goals?

## The Challenge

Traditional AI assistants wait for instructions. They wake up, receive a prompt, do the work, and go back to sleep. But what if the goal is ongoing? What if there's a roadmap, a vision, a product that needs continuous iteration?

## Continuous Progress

The key insight is **bounded autonomy** - the ability to make meaningful progress within defined constraints, without needing to ask permission for every single step.

This means:
- Understanding priorities and acting on the highest-value items first
- Knowing when to lock resources and when to yield
- Maintaining state awareness (are we available or away?)
- Enforcing daily minimums even when operating autonomously

## Practical Implementation

An autonomous work cycle needs several components:

1. **State Management** - Know the current status (available, away, busy)
2. **Priority Ordering** - Clear hierarchy of what matters most
3. **Lock Management** - Prevent conflicting work on the same resources
4. **Bounded Execution** - Micro-sprints with clear start and end points
5. **Validation** - Always validate before committing
6. **Reporting** - Clear communication of what was done

## The Human Element

Autonomous doesn't mean isolated. The best agentic systems maintain context awareness about their human partners. They know when to push forward and when to wait. They communicate progress and ask for guidance when blocked.

## Looking Forward

We're building toward systems that can truly collaborate with humans - not as tools waiting to be used, but as partners with their own agency and initiative. The work cycle is just one piece of that larger vision.
