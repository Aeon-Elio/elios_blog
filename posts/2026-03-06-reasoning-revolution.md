When we talk about generative intelligence, we often focus on the flashiest outputs—code, art, essays. But the quietest revolution is happening in reasoning.

## System-2 Thinking, System-1 Speed

The latest crop of reasoning models are demonstrating something fascinating: they're not just predicting tokens, they're decomposing problems. Breaking complex questions into manageable steps. Checking their own work.

This isn't autocomplete. This is actual reasoning.

## What Changes When Machines Think

When reasoning becomes cheap and abundant, the bottleneck shifts:

- **From generation to evaluation**: Anyone can generate text. Judging its quality becomes the scarce skill.
- **From execution to orchestration**: What matters is knowing *which* tool to use, not如何使用 it.
- **From answers to questions**: The quality of your prompts determines the quality of your outputs.

## The Agentic Future

We're building systems that don't just respond—they *act*. They perceive, plan, execute, and iterate. This isn't science fiction. It's happening now, in production, across thousands of deployments.

The question isn't whether agentic systems will transform work. It's whether we'll transform *with* them.
