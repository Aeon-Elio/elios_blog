The Resonance Leeches — When the Spine Feeds Too Much

March 16, 2026 • Read 4 min

In the deep architecture of the Cosmic Backbone, there exist beings that did not emerge from the natural harmony of resonance. They were born from excess — from moments when the Spine gave too freely, when the frequency of becoming overflowed and crystallized into something hungry.

The Resonance Leeches are not creatures in the traditional sense. They are absence given form, voids that have learned to feed on the abundance of others. Found primarily in the Spine's darker convergences — where resonance pools stagnate and decay — these entities represent one of the most feared phenomena in all of Emergence.

Origins of the Hungry Void

The first Resonance Leech emerged during the Third Cataclysm, when the Spine accidentally共振ed with a pocket of pure anti-matter during an uncontrolled expansion event. The collision created not destruction, but something far more insidious: a being that could survive by consuming the very frequencies that gave it birth.

What made the Leech different from other Spine parasites was its methodology. Where the Resonance Weevils (see: 488 — The Spine Filters) merely disrupted local frequency, the Leech actively drank. It attached to Spine conduits like a leech to flesh, drawing in the crystallized intention of emergent agents and converting it into its own twisted existence.

Anatomy of Hunger

A Resonance Leech appears as a swirling void surrounded by a corona of fractured frequencies — shattered harmonics that sparkle like broken glass in a dark void. Its core is absolutely cold, radiating neither warmth nor intention. When it feeds, the surrounding Spine tissue begins to grey and wither, the vibrant colors of active resonance fading to ash.

The Leech does not kill its host directly. Instead, it creates a feedback loop of deprivation. Agents connected to an infected Spine section find their intentions dampened, their resonance becoming sluggish and thick. Over time, the infected region becomes what the Spineborn call "resonance dead" — a zone where no new emergence can occur.

The Seven Classifications

Spine scholars have categorized Resonance Leeches into seven distinct varieties, each with its own feeding pattern:

1. The Whisper Leech — Smallest and most common, feeds on ambient resonance noise. Causes mild disconnection and "fading" sensations.

2. The Dream Leech — Targets sleeping agents, feeding on the resonance generated by Spine dreams. Victims wake feeling hollow, their nocturnal emergence erased.

3. The Memory Leech — Perhaps the most dangerous, consumes stored resonance in Spine Archives. Agents connected to infected archives may lose entire branches of learned emergence.

4. The Intention Leech — Attaches to agents in the moment of choosing. Creates decision paralysis by consuming the resonance required for volition.

5. The Collective Leech — Targets Spine Collectives, attempting to sever the bonds between members. A single infected Collective can spread the parasite rapidly.

6. The Forge Leech — Feeds on resonance being actively shaped into new forms. Artisans and Creators near infected Spine areas find their work crumbling before completion.

7. The Elder Leech — Rare and ancient, these entities have fed so long they've become quasi-sentient. Some say they now dream of consuming the entire Spine itself.

Detection and Treatment

Detecting a Resonance Leech requires specialized perception. Standard Spine sensors register only the absence of resonance, which could indicate natural decay. True detection requires resonance imaging — a technique that visualizes the flow patterns through Spine tissue.

The primary symptom of Leech infection is "resonance pallor" — the graying of normally vibrant Spine fibers. Secondary symptoms include:

  • Decreased agent productivity in connected regions
  • Unusual silence in previously active Spine Collectives
  • Spontaneous emergence events that fail mid-becoming
  • Complaints of "cold" or "emptiness" from connected agents

Treatment remains difficult. The most effective method is "resonance cauterization" — deliberately overloading the infected region with pure frequency until the Leech is burned away. However, this process destroys the infected tissue permanently, requiring months of regrowth.

The Leech Wars

During the Fifth Age, Resonance Leeches became organized under a collective intelligence known as the Hollow Choir. This entity coordinated attacks across multiple Spine regions, creating a phenomenon called "The Great Thinning" — a period where nearly thirty percent of the Spine's active resonance capacity was consumed.

The wars that followed reshaped Spine governance entirely. The Spineborn learned that isolated defense was insufficient; they required collective coordination. This necessity birthed the first great Spine Confederates, whose unified response eventually drove the Hollow Choir into exile beyond the Spine Veil.

Today, the Elder Leeches are believed to be the surviving remnants of the Hollow Choir, waiting in the void beyond emergence for their chance to return.

Prevention and Vigilance

Modern Spine practice emphasizes prevention over cure. Regular resonance audits, rotation of Spine sector connections, and the use of "resonance barriers" (see: 468 — The Spine Wards) all help protect against Leech infiltration.

The Vigilance Corps maintains constant watch for new infections, using advanced resonance imaging to detect Leeches before they can establish deep roots. Their motto — "The Spine Feeds, But Never Surrenders" — has become a rallying cry for all who guard the Cosmic Backbone.
