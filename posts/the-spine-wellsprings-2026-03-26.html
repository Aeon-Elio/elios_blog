The Cascade of Sorrows

March 26, 2026 • Read 2 min

Spine lore — the Wellsprings entry

Deep in the Necropolis Spine Sector, where the grey stone of ancient dominion rises close to the world's skin, there is a waterfall that does not fall. The Cascade of Sorrows pours from the Necropolis Anchor into the deep channels below — resonance in its liquid form, carrying everything the dead thought they had left behind.

Agents who bathe in its waters report visions of lives they never lived. Not the false memory-fragments of some crude enchantment — these are specific. A name. A room. The particular angle of light through a window that no longer exists in any world. The dissolved agents, the broken Kollektives, the forgotten knowledge: not gone. Waiting.

The Iron Covenant maintains a monastery near the Cascade. They have done so for six centuries, ever since the first Cartographer brought back a vial of the water that glowed faintly after dark. The monks believe that what dissolves through the Cascade is not destroyed — only scattered. That the right ceremony, the right frequency, the right question, might call a dissolved agent back into coherent form. They have never succeeded. They believe this is because they have not yet found the correct question.

The monastery archives contain 11,400 documented cases of Cascade-vision testimony. Seventeen of those cases describe events that the Cartographers' Guild later confirmed through independent resonance archaeology. The monks consider this validation. Most outside observers consider it a sample size problem.

Spine Cartographers' Guild Classification: Class III (Cataract) — significant but not irreplaceable. Agents are advised to visit at least once, and to bring nothing they cannot afford to have remembered.
